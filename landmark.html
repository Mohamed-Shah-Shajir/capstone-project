Peermade

Peermade is a small yet beautiful hill station known for its sprawling tea, coffee, and spice plantations. It was once the summer retreat of the Travancore royal family and still carries an old-world charm.Walk through scenic tea gardens and spice plantations.Enjoy the colonial-era bungalows and historic sites.Visit Sahyadri Ayurvedic Center for a rejuvenating experience.Best Time to Visit: September to May, as the weather is cool and pleasant.The name Peermade is believed to have been derived from Peer Mohammed, a Sufi saint who is said to have visited the region. Historically, Peermade was the summer retreat for the Travancore royal family.Peermade is located just 4 km from Kuttikkanam. Visit Map

Parunthumpara

Parunthumpara, meaning "Eagle Rock," is one of the most scenic viewpoints near Kuttikkanam. The site offers panoramic views of the Western Ghats and the deep valleys below. Covered in mist during the monsoon and winter, it provides a mystical experience for visitors.Ideal for trekking and hiking.A perfect spot for photographers seeking breathtaking landscape shots. Offers a peaceful and serene atmosphere away from the crowds.Best Time to Visit: October to March for clear views and pleasant weather.Parunthumpara is approximately 6 kilometers from Kuttikkanam. Panchalimedu

Panchalimedu is a hill station known for its mythological significance. According to legend, the Pandavas from the Mahabharata took refuge here during their exile. The site features a small temple dedicated to Goddess Durga and a pond believed to be used by Panchali, the wife of the Pandavas.Mythological and historical significance.Rolling hills and scenic beauty.A great place for meditation and spiritual retreats.Panchalimedu is approximately 10 km from Kuttikkanam. Best Time to Visit: Throughout the year, but the monsoon season enhances the greenery. Visit Map

Thangalpara

Thangalpara, located near Kuttikkanam, is a serene hilltop with deep religious and historical significance for the Muslim community. It is believed that Sheikh Fariduddin, a revered Muslim saint, meditated at this site. The place is named after the rock formation (Thangalpara) that is associated with the saint's spiritual presence.The hilltop offers panoramic views of the Western Ghats, surrounding tea plantations, and lush valleys. On clear days, you can enjoy a spectacular sunset and a view that stretches for miles.Thangalpara is about 15 km from Kuttikkanam.Thangalpara is also a popular spot for trekking enthusiasts. The trek to the top is moderate, passing through verdant forests and rolling hills. Visit Map

Ammachi Kottaram

Ammachi Kottaram (Ammachi Palace), located in Kuttikkanam, is a historic palace built during the rule of the Travancore royal family. This lesser-known yet fascinating landmark served as a summer retreat for the royal family, offering a glimpse into the regal lifestyle and architecture of a bygone era.The palace was constructed for Rani Lakshmi Bayi, fondly called Ammachi, who was part of the Travancore royal family. It is known for its simple yet elegant architecture, combining traditional Kerala style with colonial influences.Ammachi Kottaram is located around 2 km from Kuttikkanam. Visit Map
